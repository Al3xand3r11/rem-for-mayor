Bowie Forward: Respecting Bowie’s legacy while preparing its future.  
I am a lifelong Bowie resident and graduate of Bowie State University who believes the future of our city depends on investing in our young people and making sure every resident has a voice.   
**Big 3 Policies:**

1. Youth Opportunity and Development

Bowie should be a city where every young person has a mentor, an opportunity, and a path forward.  
**Empowering Bowie’s Next Generation**

- Expand after-school programs, mentorship, and career exploration partnerships with Bowie State and local businesses.  
- Establish a Mayor’s Youth Advisory Council to empower teens in shaping city decisions.  
- Increase summer jobs and internship opportunities for Bowie youth.  
- Invest in mental health resources and youth counseling partnerships in schools and community centers.

2. Community Engagement and Transparent Government

I want to make sure every voice in Bowie is not only heard, but truly considered. Our seniors helped build this community, and I will work to keep them connected, engaged, and respected.  
**Strengthening Community Engagement**

- Monthly community listening sessions.  
- Launch a digital civic engagement platform so residents can easily share concerns and vote on small community improvements.  
- Expand participatory budgeting for neighborhood projects.

3. Safety, Support for Local Businesses, and Overall Growth

I want to keep Bowie safe, vibrant, and community-centered, and protect what residents already love about Bowie.  
**Growth & Safety**

- Small business grants for Bowie entrepreneurs.  
- Partnerships with Bowie State to create local innovation and startup pipelines.  
- Protect green spaces and parks.  
- Improve traffic flow and pedestrian safety.  
- Continue investments in recreation centers, youth sports, and cultural events.

Only 15% of registered voters participated in Bowie’s last mayoral election. I want to change that by engaging all residents. Through programs like a Mayor’s Youth Advisory Council for the youth, and through opportunities to stay connected for seniors. When more people participate, Bowie becomes a stronger, more representative community

# announcement letter

My name is Remington Tennessee. I’m a proud lifelong resident of Bowie, a graduate of Bowie State University, and someone who truly believes in the future of our city, which is why I am announcing my campaign for Mayor of Bowie.

Bowie is more than just where I live. It’s where I grew up, learned about community, and discovered the importance of mentorship, opportunity, and service. Like many of you, my story connects to the neighborhoods, schools, and people that make this city special.

I’m running because I believe our city’s future depends on how well we engage the community and how intentionally we invest in our young people.

As a graduate of Bowie State University and a student currently pursuing a master’s degree in school counseling, I’ve dedicated my path to supporting young people and helping them find direction, opportunity, and purpose. That work has reinforced something I’ve always believed: when young people succeed, our whole community succeeds.

As mayor, I want to make Bowie a leading city for youth opportunities. This means expanding mentorship programs, increasing access to summer jobs and internships, and developing a Mayor’s Youth Advisory Council so the next generation has a voice in shaping our city’s future.

But this campaign is about more than just youth programs. It’s about building a city government that really listens to its residents. I aim to host regular community listening sessions, enhance transparency at City Hall, and ensure that every neighborhood feels heard and represented.

I also believe Bowie should continue to support small businesses, protect our green spaces, and invest in safe, vibrant neighborhoods where families can thrive.

Our city has amazing potential. To realize that potential, we need leadership that is grounded in the community and focused on the future.

I’m not running just because I want to hold office. I’m running because I care deeply about this city and the people who call it home.

Bowie helped shape who I am. Now I want to help shape Bowie’s future.

Together, we can build a city where every resident feels heard, every neighborhood feels connected, and every young person knows they have a place and a future here.

Thank you. I look forward to working with all of you to move Bowie forward.

# Forum question

**What options would you pursue to improve the economic situation in**  
**Bowie? (Examples can be: employment opportunities, shopping**  
**opportunities, other services such as restaurants, as well as the**  
**continuing (new) development**

To strengthen the economy in Bowie, I would focus on supporting local businesses and creating more opportunities for residents.

First, we should make it easier for small businesses to start and grow by exploring tools like small business grants and promoting local shopping initiatives, whether that be for produce or services. I would also look at ways to strengthen the farmers' market, including experimenting with weekend operating days and potentially relocating it to an area with more natural foot traffic, such as near Allen Pond Park, so more residents can support local vendors.

Second, we should strengthen partnerships with Bowie State University to create more internships, job opportunities, and entrepreneurship pathways for students and young professionals. I would also explore pilot programs like an NSBE Jr. chapter and a Mayor’s Youth Advisory Council at Bowie High to connect after-school programs with real career opportunities.

And of course, economic growth also depends on maintaining a safe and stable community where businesses and families feel confident investing their time and resources.

Overall, my goal is thoughtful growth that creates jobs, supports local businesses, and keeps Bowie a safe and vibrant place to live, work, and visit.

# News questions

**Why Mayor**

I decided to run for mayor of Bowie because this community has been home to me for over 20 years. I attended Pointer Ridge Elementary School and later graduated from Bowie State University, where I’m currently pursuing my master’s. I’ve also interned and taught at Bowie High School, so Bowie has truly shaped who I am. With my background in education and school counseling, I’ve been on the front lines seeing what our young people need. I see leadership as service: advocating for youth, supporting families, and making sure every resident feels heard. I’m running because I want to help guide Bowie’s future while honoring and respecting the legacy of the community that helped raise me. I’m not here to sell a dream or make empty promises. I’m here to be a voice for every Bowie resident, to listen, advocate, and work toward real solutions that strengthen our community

**Housing Affordability / Cost of Living**

In Bowie, many families are feeling the pressure of rising housing costs, and we have to be thoughtful about how we respond. I support responsible development that includes a range of housing options so young families, working professionals, and seniors can continue living here. At the same time, strengthening our local economy helps with affordability. That’s why I want to support small businesses and local markets, like exploring ways to grow the farmers market so residents have more accessible, local options.

**Families Worried About Youth Entering the System**

As someone studying school counseling, I believe prevention and opportunity are key. When young people have mentorship, after-school programs, and leadership opportunities, they’re much less likely to enter the justice system. I’d like to pilot initiatives like an NSBE Jr. chapter and a Mayor’s Youth Advisory Council at Bowie High, so students have more pathways into leadership, STEM, and community involvement.

**Jobs and Economic Opportunity**

Economic opportunity is really about connecting people to pathways. One opportunity Bowie has is its relationship with Bowie State University. I want to strengthen partnerships that create internships, entrepreneurship opportunities, and job pathways so students and young professionals can build their careers right here in Bowie.

**City Services and Quality of Life**

Residents expect strong services and a safe community, and that should always remain a priority. My goal is to make sure our city services continue supporting families at every stage of life—from youth programs and recreation opportunities to community engagement for seniors. When people feel supported and connected, it strengthens the entire community.
